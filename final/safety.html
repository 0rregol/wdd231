Earthquake Safety Information

Before an Earthquake

  • Prepare an emergency kit with water, food, flashlight, and first aid supplies
  • Secure heavy furniture and appliances to walls
  • Identify safe spots in each room (under sturdy tables, against interior walls)
  • Practice drop, cover, and hold on drills with your family
  • Develop a family emergency plan and designated meeting points

During an Earthquake

  • Drop to your hands and knees to prevent being knocked over
  • Cover your head and neck with your arms, take cover under sturdy furniture
  • Hold on to any sturdy shelter until shaking stops
  • If indoors, stay there - don't run outside
  • If in bed, stay there and protect your head with a pillow
  • Stay clear of windows, tall furniture, and light fixtures

After an Earthquake

  • Check yourself and others for injuries
  • Be prepared for aftershocks
  • If in a damaged building, go outside carefully
  • Only use phones for emergencies
  • Listen to official information sources for instructions
  • If you smell gas, turn off the main gas valve and evacuate

Emergency Kit Checklist

Your Earthquake Emergency Kit

  • Water (4 liters per person per day for 3 days)
  • Non-perishable food (3-day supply for each person)
  • First aid kit, medications, and prescription glasses
  • Flashlight and extra batteries (LED recommended)
  • Whistle to signal for help
  • Dust masks and plastic sheeting/duct tape (to shelter-in-place)
  • Manual can opener (if food includes canned goods)
  • Wrench or pliers (to turn off utilities)
  • Local maps and emergency contact information (paper copies)
  • Battery-powered or hand-crank radio
  • Cell phone with chargers and backup battery
  • Personal hygiene items (wet wipes, hand sanitizer)
  • Important family documents (copies in waterproof bag)
  • Cash in small denominations
  • Sleeping bag or warm blanket for each person
  • Matches in a waterproof container
  • Change of clothes for each person
